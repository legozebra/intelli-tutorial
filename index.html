When you think of programmers, who do you think of? Russians? Chinese? Americans?

You probably don't think of Africans. But if only there

was a way to get Africa into the technology world

Welcome to the Kangemi Slums of Nairobi, Kenya.

Located here is a school.

Hamomi Children's Center seeks to improve the lives of orphaned and vulnerable children by developing them into self-sustaining adults

I'll let their website, Hamomi.org, elaborate.

We believe that the knowledge of technology is essential

for all children throughout the world to be skilled at.

In this age, technology is what's "big." But fast forward 10, or even just 5 years from now. Will the technology we teach be obsolete by then?

The simple truth is that Windows 7 will be obsolete in just a few years.

It is already fading away. But our goal isn't to teach these kids what

the "big thing" is today.

Our goal is to make them autodidacts - we want them to teach

themselves about whatever the "next big thing" is through the

resources they have available. In this case, it would be Google.

This is why our tutorial concludes by teaching these students how to use Google to their advantage to learn whatever they want. The internet is almost endless. It is up to

us - the students of this age of technology - to shape the future.

The future is what we make it.

ABOUT US

 

IntelliTutorial is all about raising children out of the seemingly-endless cycle of poverty that accompanies life in the slums. This is why we have partnered with Hamomi Children's Center to create a comprehensive program that taught kids in the slums how to use computers. Our goal? By the end of the tutorials, the children should be able to auto-didactically expand their knowledge for years to come.
